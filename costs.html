What the wall actually costs.

The campaign goal is $5,000. The full build costs more than that — this page is the under-the-hood math: the wall, the holds, the pads, the shipping, the duty, the install. Ranges in USD; every number carries its source or is marked as an estimate. None of this promises a wall. It prices one.

Three ways to build it

Same idea at three price points: a wall, a set of Kilter holds, pads on the floor. Option B is the one we'd argue for — a real adjustable Lemur wall without the full commercial system.

What any candidate room must have

  • Ceiling height: 10.8–15.4 ft (3.3–4.7 m) depending on model — a normal-tall room, not a hall.
  • Floor: concrete slab; ~90% of Lemur installs use 10 heavy-duty anchors straight into it.
  • Power: 100–240V universal — a wall socket. No special electrical work.
  • Clearance: pad coverage extending well past the fall zone; desks and furniture far from the board.

Source: Lemur Design FAQ. Lead time is 8–12 weeks manufacture; install is 1–2 days for the structure plus a day for holds.

What $5,000 gets us

The escrow is priced demand plus seed capital — not the whole build. Here's the honest mapping of the goal against each option, and who covers the rest.

Under Option A

$5,000 of a $5,700 low-end build — the community's escrow nearly builds the entire thing. Remainder: $700–5,800, closeable by a small top-up or a second round.

Under Option B

$5,000 covers the holds, the pads, and the install — roughly everything except the wall itself and its shipping. Remainder: $6,000–18,400, which falls to Network School or a sponsor.

Under Option C

$5,000 is seed capital — about 10% of the low end. This option is a capital decision for Network School; the escrow is the demand proof attached to it.

Either way the mechanics hold: deposits stay withdrawable until release, and release needs the goal and the greenlight. If the wall isn't approved, every cent goes back. The number this page can't produce is how much the community wants it — the counter on the main page is doing that.
