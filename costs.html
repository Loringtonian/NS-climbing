climbing at Network School cost breakdown

What the wall actually costs.

There is no fundraising target — the campaign raises what it raises, and every dollar raised goes to the build. This page is the under-the-hood math it chips away at. The plan is committed: full commercial Lemur, the widest board in the lineup, manual adjustment, into the existing gym. No half measures. Ranges in USD; every number carries its source or is marked as an estimate. None of this promises a wall. It prices one.

The system

Kilter Original 16×12 on a Lemur adjustable frame — manual.

16 feet wide, 12 feet tall: the largest layout Kilter makes. Wide enough for several climbers at once, with the full Original hold set — 646 holds, 1,300 LEDs, the complete app catalogue from beginner to world-cup. The frame is Lemur's adjustable steel, vertical to steep. Manual adjustment instead of motors: same angles, fewer parts, less to break, less to pay.

One honest caveat: Lemur's current commercial line ships motorized (electric frame from $20,700); a manual frame at 16-ft width is a quote conversation, priced below the electric anchor. The formal Lemur quote is THE open item — every number below is ± that quote.

What any candidate room must have

  • Width: the board is 16 ft wide — the room wants ~18+ ft of clear wall.
  • Ceiling height: 13.3–15.4 ft (4.1–4.7 m) for Lemur's full-size adjustable walls.
  • Floor: concrete slab; ~90% of Lemur installs use 10 heavy-duty anchors straight into it.
  • Power: 100–240V wall socket for the LEDs. Manual frame — no motor circuit at all.
  • Clearance: pad coverage extending well past the fall zone; desks and furniture far from the board.

Source: Lemur Design FAQ + commercial wall specs. Lead time is 8–12 weeks manufacture; install is 1–2 days for the structure plus a day for holds.

Where the money goes

There is no goal and no cap. Every dollar raised goes to the build — the escrow raises what it raises, and whatever lands is the community's opening bid against a $51,862–72,362 build ($66,862–87,362 if a room is needed).

The honest split

Whatever the community escrows chips directly off that number; the remainder is a capital decision for Network School, with the raise attached to it as demand proof. Every dollar in the pot is a considered, locked-in yes — which is worth more per dollar than any signature list.

The mechanics hold throughout: deposits lock into a pool no person holds, and the pool can only ever do three things — fund the build (depositor majority + organizer agree on the destination), refund everyone (majority votes dissolve), or refund everyone (the 90-day timer runs out). No individual take-backs; nobody can move a cent alone. The number this page can't produce is how much the community wants it — the counter on the main page is doing that.

Paths we considered

Two cheaper builds were on the table before committing to the full system: a DIY fixed-angle frame with a Kilter Full Ride kit ($5,700–10,800 landed) and a small Lemur manual wall with the same kit ($11,000–23,400 landed). Both work; both are half measures — a 7×10 board is a queue, not a gym. The decision went to the widest board Lemur will sell us.
